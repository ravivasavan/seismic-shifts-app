# ADR-0001: Diptych structure

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-05-06

## Context

Seismic Shifts began as a single screen-based work that listens to a
room and renders its acoustic energy as a slowly scrolling line. As
the conceptual frame deepened — from "presence of a room" to "seismic
moments in the history of Deaf life and signed languages" — a single
piece could not hold both the room-scale present-tense reading and
the civilisational arc that gives the title its second meaning.

## Decision

The work is a two-part seismogram presented together as a diptych.

- **The digital trace.** A screen-based work that listens to the room
  and draws its acoustic energy in real time. One mic, one line, one
  slow scroll.
- **The hand-drawn long arc.** An ink piece on A0 paper that traces
  the presence of Deaf life and signed languages from 65,000 BP to
  today, pockmarked by the seismic moments that have shaped and
  unmade that presence.

Materials line: *Software, microphone, screen; ink on A0 paper.*

## Considered alternatives

- **Single piece, digital only.** Cannot hold the deep-time framing.
  The trace becomes a metaphor without an anchor.
- **Two unrelated works under different titles.** Loses the shared
  seismograph metaphor and the play between *seismic shifts* as
  acoustic events in a room and *seismic shifts* as historical
  events that broke a line.
- **Diptych** (chosen). Same metaphor, two scales of time. Each
  piece reads on its own; together they argue.

## Consequences

- The site needs a public surface for the long-arc piece. See
  [ADR-0004](./0004-long-arc-as-web-page.md).
- Visual language must be shared across the two pieces — palette,
  typography, and specific motifs. See
  [ADR-0006](./0006-cross-piece-visual-echoes.md).
- The catalogue/wall-label text leads with "two-part seismogram" so
  the diptych structure is immediately legible to a reader who has
  only seen the text.
- Future iteration should keep the two pieces synchronised. If the
  digital piece changes its visual vocabulary, the drawing follows.
