# ADR-0003: The presence claim — hear, see, or feel

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-05-06

## Context

Seismic Shifts has a central claim: *what is recorded is real — a
room has presence, with or without anyone to hear it.* This claim
sits at the heart of the wall label, catalogue entry, and footer
copy across the site.

Initially the claim only said *to hear it.* That privileged hearing
audiences in the metaphysics of the work — odd for a piece explicitly
made for both Deaf and hearing audiences. The artist added *or see
it,* recognising that the visualisation makes the room present to
Deaf audiences through a different sense.

Then a further extension: *or feel it,* for blind and low-vision
audiences who register a room not as image or sound but as vibration,
air movement, and the tactile field of an acoustic environment.

## Decision

The claim now reads, in two parts:

> A room has presence, with or without anyone to hear, see, or feel
> it. A language has presence, with or without an institution willing
> to recognise it.

The first sentence belongs to the room; the second to the long arc
of the work's argument about Deaf life and signed languages. The two
sentences travel together — wall label, catalogue, site index, arc
page (until the footer was removed).

## Considered alternatives

- **"to hear it" only** — privileges hearing; excludes Deaf audiences
  from the metaphysics.
- **"to hear or see it"** — includes Deaf and hearing but not blind
  or low-vision audiences.
- **"to hear, see, or feel it"** (chosen) — registers presence as a
  sensory commons. Blind and low-vision viewers are included not by
  being signed at, but by the acoustic field reaching them as touch.

## Consequences

- Updated across `docs/artwork-description.md` (wall label and
  catalogue), `seismic-shifts-site/index.html`, the (now-removed) arc
  page footer, and both repos' READMEs.
- The claim is metaphysical, not feature-list — the current work is
  visual; this language does not promise haptic output. A future
  iteration may add a transducer attached to the frame; for now the
  claim asks the *room* to do the work, not the artwork's hardware.
- The audience line — *"For both Deaf and hearing audiences"* —
  speaks to who the visualisation is for. The presence claim speaks
  to who the room is for. The two should not be conflated.
